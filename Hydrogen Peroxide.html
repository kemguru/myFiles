Hydrogen Peroxide

Practise balancing equations:

Direction:

  • In the following equations you will see a blank box before the symbol of each compound and element. Enter the lowest possible number in each box, or leave the box blank if you think the chemical needn't have a number (i.e. the number is equivalent to '1').
  • When you think the equation is balanced, click on the small button "Check answer" that appears below it.
  • If your equation is incorrect, then click the button "Reset" and try balancing the equation again.
  • In case you are in a hurry or are unable to balance, then click the button "Show answer."

  • Preparation of Hydrogen Peroxide:

    1. Hydrogen peroxide is prepared by the reaction of Barium peroxide with Phosphoric acid:

      2. BaO2 + H3PO4 → Ba3(PO4)2 + H2O2

               

    2. Hydrogen peroxide is also prepared by the reaction of Sodium peroxide with ice cold Sulphuric acid:

      3. Na2O2 + H2SO4 → Na2SO4 + H2O2

               

  • Reactions of Hydrogen Peroxide:

    • Oxidizing Properties:

      Hydrogen peroxide oxidizes:

      1. Potassium iodide into iodine.

        2. H2O2 + KI → I2 + KOH

                 

      2. Black lead(II) sulphide into white Lead(II) sulphate.

        3. H2O2 + PbS → PbSO4 + H2O

                 

      3. Acidified Iron(II)sulphate into Iron(III) sulphate.

        4. H2O2 + FeSO4 + H2SO4 → Fe2(SO4)3 + H2O

                 

      4. Sodium sulphite into Sodium sulphate.

        5. H2O2 + Na2SO3 → Na2SO4 + H2O

                 

      5. Potassium nitrite into Potassium nitrate.

        6. H2O2 + KNO2 → KNO3 + H2O

                 

    • Reducing Properties:

      Hydrogen peroxide reduces:

      1. Chlorine into Hydrochloric acid.

        2. H2O2 + Cl2 → O2 + HCl

                 

      2. Ozone into Oxygen.

        3. H2O2 + O3 → H2O + O2

                 

      3. Acidified Potassium permanganate into Manganous salt.

        4. H2O2 + KMnO4 H2SO4 → K2SO4 + MnSO4 + H2O + O2
