  • Reactions of Carbon dioxide:

    • Under high pressure Carbon dioxide gas dissolves in water to form weakly acidic Carbonic acid:

      • CO2 + H2O → H2CO3

               

    • Burning Magnesium continues to burn in Carbon dioxide gas to form Magnesium oxide and Carbon:

      • Mg + CO2 → MgO + C

               

    • When Carbon dioxide gas is passed into lime water, first it forms milky Calcium carbonate; and when excess carbon dioxide is passed into the milky suspension, it becomes colourless Calcium bicarbonate solution :

      • CO2 + Ca(OH)2 → CaCO3 + H2O

               

      CO2 + CaCO3 H2O → Ca(HCO3)2

               

    • When Carbon dioxide gas is passed into Sodium hydroxide solution, first it forms colourless Sodium carbonate; and when excess carbon dioxide is passed into the colourless solution ,it forms a faint white suspension of Sodium bicarbonate:

      • CO2 + NaOH → Na2CO3 + H2O

               

      CO2 + Na2CO3 H2O → NaHCO3

               

    • When Carbon dioxide gas is passed over red hot coke(Carbon), Carbon monoxide gas is formed:

      • CO2 + C → CO

               

    • At 200oC temperature and 200 atm pressure Carbon dioxide gas reacts with Ammonia gas to form urea (NH2CONH2)and water:

      • CO2 + NH3 → NH2CONH2 + H2O

               

  • Effect of heat on metallic carbonates:

    • When white Calcium carbonate is heated strongly, it decomposes into white Calcium oxide and colourless Carbon dioxide gas:

      • CaCO3 → CaO + CO2

               

    • When white Magnesium carbonate is heated strongly, it decomposes into white Magnesium oxide and colourless Carbon dioxide gas:

      • MgCO3 → MgO + CO2

               

    • When white Zinc carbonate is heated strongly, it decomposes into Zinc oxide which is yellow when hot and white when cold, and colourless Carbon dioxide gas:

      • ZnCO3 → ZnO + CO2

               

    • When white Lead(II) carbonate is heated strongly, it decomposes into Lead(II) oxide which is orange when hot and yellow when cold, and colourless Carbon dioxide gas:

      • PbCO3 → PbO + CO2

               

    • When green Copper(II) carbonate is heated strongly, it decomposes into black Copper(II) oxide, and colourless Carbon dioxide gas:

      • CuCO3 → CuO + CO2

               

    • When Mercury(II) carbonate is heated gently, it decomposes into Mercury metal,colourless Carbon dioxide gas and Oxygen gas:

      • HgCO3 → Hg + CO2 + O2

               

    • When Silver carbonate is heated gently, it decomposes into Silver metal,colourless Carbon dioxide gas and Oxygen gas:

      • Ag2CO3 → Ag + CO2 + O2

               

  • Effect of heat on metallic bicarbonates:

    • When white Potassium bicarbonate is heated, it decomposes into white Potassium carbonate, Carbon dioxide gas and water:

      • KHCO3 → K2CO3 + CO2 + H2O

               

    • When white Sodium bicarbonate is heated, it decomposes into white Sodium carbonate, Carbon dioxide gas and water:

      • NaHCO3 → Na2CO3 + CO2 + H2O

               

    • When Calcium bicarbonate solution is heated, it decomposes into white Calcium carbonate, Carbon dioxide gas and water:

      • Ca(HCO3)2 → CaCO3 + CO2 + H2O

               

    • When Magnesium bicarbonate solution is heated, it decomposes into white Magnesium carbonate, Carbon dioxide gas and water:

      • Mg(HCO3)2 → MgCO3 + CO2 + H2O
