Chlorine

Practise balancing equations:

Direction:

  • In the following equations you will see a blank box before the symbol of each compound and element. Enter the lowest possible number in each box, or leave the box blank if you think the chemical needn't have a number (i.e. the number is equivalent to '1').
  • When you think the equation is balanced, click on the small button "Check answer" that appears below it.
  • If your equation is incorrect, then click the button "Reset" and try balancing the equation again.
  • In case you are in a hurry or are unable to balance, then click the button "Show answer."

Note:

If you get no response after clicking 'Check answer' or 'Show answer', your browser has disabled to run this program. Enable it by clicking below the 'address bar' and follow the instructions.

  • Preparation of Chlorine:

    1. Sodium Chloride reacts with concentrated Sulphuric acid and Manganese dioxide to form Chlorine, Sodium hydrogen sulphate, Manganese sulphate and water:

      2. NaCl + H2SO4 +MnO2→ Cl2 + NaHSO4 + MnSO4 + H2O

               

    2. Manganese dioxide when heated with concentrated hydrochloric acid, produces Manganese chloride, chlorine and water.

      3. HCl + MnO2→ MnCl2 + Cl2 + H2O

               

  • Reactions of Chlorine:

    1. Hydrogen sulphide gas reacts with Chlorine gas to form Sulphur and Hydrogen chloride gas:

      2. H2S + Cl2→ S + HCl

               

    2. Excess Ammonia reacts with Chlorine gas to form nitrogen gas and white fumes of Ammonium chloride:

      3. NH3 + Cl2 → N2 + NH4Cl

               

    3. Excess Chlorine reacts with Ammonia gas to form Nitrogen trichloride and misty fumes of Hydrogen chloride:

      4. NH3 + Cl2 → NCl3 + HCl

               

    4. Chlorine reacts with cold dilute sodium hydroxide to form sodium chloride, sodium hypochlorite and water:

      5. Cl2 + NaOH → NaCl + NaClO + H2O

               

    5. Chlorine reacts with hot concentrated sodium hydroxide to form sodium chloride, sodium chlorate and water:

      Cl2 + NaOH → NaClO3 + NaCl + H2O
