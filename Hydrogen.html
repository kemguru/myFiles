Hydrogen

Practise balancing equations:

Direction:

  • In the following equations you will see a blank box before the symbol of each compound and element. Enter the lowest possible number in each box, or leave the box blank if you think the chemical needn't have a number (i.e. the number is equivalent to '1').
  • When you think the equation is balanced, click on the small button "Check answer" that appears below it.
  • If your equation is incorrect, then click the button "Reset" and try balancing the equation again.
  • In case you are in a hurry or are unable to balance, then click the button "Show answer."

  • Preparation of Hydrogen(Laboratory Method):

    • Zinc granules react with dilute Sulphuric acid to form zinc sulphate and Hydrogen:

      • Zn + H2SO4 → ZnSO4 + H2

               

    • Zinc granules react with dilute Hydrochloric acid to form Zinc chloride and Hydrogen:

      • Zn + HCl → ZnCl2 + H2

               

  • Preparation of Hydrogen(Other methods):

    • By the action of active metals on cold water:

      • Potassium metal reacts with cold water to form Potassium hydroxide and Hydrogen.

        • K + H2O → KOH + H2

                 

      • Sodium metal reacts with cold water to form Sodium hydroxide and Hydrogen.

        • Na + H2O → NaOH + H2

                 

      • Calcium metal reacts with cold water to form Calcium hydroxide and Hydrogen.

        • Ca + H2O → Ca(OH)2 + H2

                 

    • By the action of steam (H2O) on metals:

      • Magnesium metal reacts with steam to form Magnesium oxide and Hydrogen.

        • Mg + H2O → MgO + H2

                 

      • Zinc metal reacts with steam to form Zinc oxide and Hydrogen.

        • Zn + H2O → ZnO + H2

                 

      • Iron metal reacts with steam to form Tri iron tetroxide and Hydrogen.

        • Fe + H2O    Fe3O4 + H2

                 

    • By the action of acids on metals:

      • Magnesium metal reacts with dilute Sulphuric acid to form Magnesium sulphate and Hydrogen.

        • Mg + H2SO4 → MgSO4 + H2

                 

      • Magnesium metal reacts with dilute Hydrochloric acid to form Magnesium chloride and Hydrogen:

        • Mg + HCl → MgCl2 + H2

                 

      • Aluminium metal reacts with dilute Sulphuric acid to form Aluminium sulphate and Hydrogen.

        • Al + H2SO4 → Al2(SO4)3 + H2

                 

      • Aluminium metal reacts with dilute Hydrochloric acid to form Aluminium chloride and Hydrogen:

        • Al + HCl → AlCl3 + H2

                 

      • Iron metal reacts with dilute Sulphuric acid to form Iron(II) sulphate and Hydrogen.

        • Fe + H2SO4 → FeSO4 + H2

                 

      • Iron metal reacts with dilute Hydrochloric acid to form Iron(II) chloride and Hydrogen:

        • Fe + HCl → FeCl2 + H2

                 

      • Magnesium metal reacts with very dilute Nitric acid to form Magnesium nitrate and Hydrogen:

        • Mg + HNO3 → Mg(NO3)2 + H2

                 

    • By the action of alkalis on metals:

      • Zinc metal reacts with concentrated Sodium hydroxide solution to form Sodium zincate and Hydrogen:

        • Zn + NaOH → Na2ZnO2 + H2

                 

      • Zinc metal reacts with concentrated Potassium hydroxide solution to form Potassium zincate and Hydrogen:

        • Zn + KOH → K2ZnO2 + H2

                 

      • Lead metal reacts with concentrated Sodium hydroxide solution to form Sodium plumbite and Hydrogen:

        • Pb + NaOH → Na2PbO2 + H2

                 

      • Lead metal reacts with concentrated Potassium hydroxide solution to form Potassium plumbite and Hydrogen:

        • Pb + KOH → K2PbO2 + H2

                 

      • Aluminium metal reacts with concentrated Sodium hydroxide solution to form Sodium aluminate and Hydrogen:

        • Al + NaOH + H2O → NaAlO2 + H2

                 

      • Aluminium metal reacts with concentrated Potassium hydroxide solution to form Potassium aluminate and Hydrogen:

        • Al + KOH + H2O → KAlO2 + H2

                 

  • Manufacture of Hydrogen:

    • In Bosch Process, white hot coke at about 1000oC is reacted with steam when water gas(CO + H2) is formed:

      • C + H2O → CO + H2

               

    • In Modern Process,a mixture of Methane (present in natural gas) and steam is passed over heated Nickel(catalyst)at about 800oC when synthesis gas(CO + 3 H2) is formed:

      • CH4 + H2O → CO + H2

               

    • Next,the mixture of Carbon monoxide and Hydrogen is mixed with more steam and passed over heated Iron(III)oxide(catalyst) at 450oC when Carbon dioxide and Hydrogen are formed:

      • CO + H2 + H2O → CO2 + H2

               

    • Carbon dioxide is removed by passing the mixture through Potassium hydroxide solution when Carbon dioxide reacts with Potassium hydroxide forming Potassium carbonate and water:

      • CO2 + KOH → K2CO3 + H2O

               

  • Reactions of Hydrogen:

    • A mixture of Hydrogen and Oxygen, when ignited, burns with a 'pop' sound to form water:

      • H2 + O2 → H2O

               

    • In diffused sunlight Hydrogen reacts with Chlorine to form Hydrogen chloride gas:

      • H2 + Cl2 → HCl

               

    • When bubbled through molten Sulphur, Hydrogen gas reacts with Sulphur to form Hydrogen sulphide gas:

      • H2 + S → H2S

               

    • When A mixture of Nitrogen gas and Hydrogen gas is passed over Iron catalyst at 200 atm pressure and 450oC, Ammonia gas is formed:

      • N2 + H2     NH3

              

    • When Hydrogen gas is passed over heated black Copper(II) oxide, brown Copper and water are formed:

      H2 + CuO → Cu + H2O

               

    • When Hydrogen gas is passed over heated yellow Lead(II) oxide, grey Lead and water are formed:

      • H2 + PbO → Pb + H2O

               

    • When Hydrogen gas is passed over heated brown Iron(III) oxide, grey Iron and water are formed:

      H2 + Fe2O3 → Fe + H2O
