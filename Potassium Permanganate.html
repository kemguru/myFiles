Potassium Permanganate

Practise balancing equations:

Direction:

  • In the following equations you will see a blank box before the symbol of each compound and element. Enter the lowest possible number in each box, or leave the box blank if you think the chemical needn't have a number (i.e. the number is equivalent to '1').
  • When you think the equation is balanced, click on the small button "Check answer" that appears below it.
  • If your equation is incorrect, then click the button "Reset" and try balancing the equation again.
  • In case you are in a hurry or are unable to balance, then click the button "Show answer."

Note:

If you get no response after clicking 'Check answer' or 'Show answer', your browser has disabled to run this program. Enable it by clicking below the 'address bar' and follow the instructions.

  • Extraction of Potassium permanganate from pyrolusite ore:

    • The process involves the following two steps:

    • Conversion of pyrolusite to potassium manganate:

      • The pyrolusite mineral(MnO2) is finely powdered and fused with potassium hydroxide or potassium carbonate in the presence of air, when a green mass of potassium manganate is obtained.

      MnO2 + KOH + O2 → K2MnO4 + H2O

               

      MnO2 + K2CO3 + O2 → K2MnO4 + CO2

               

    • Oxidation of potassium manganate into potassium permanganate:

      The fused mass obtained from the above step is extracted with water when a green solution of potassium manganate is obtained.It is now treated with a current of carbon dioxide or chlorine or ozone , when potassium manganate is oxidized into potassium permanganate.

      • K2MnO4 + CO2→ KMnO4 + MnO2 +K2CO3

               

      K2MnO4 + Cl2→ KMnO4 + KCl

               

      K2MnO4 + O3 + H2O→ KMnO4 + O2 +KOH

               

  • Oxidizing reactions of Potassium permanganate:

    • In acidic medium:

      In the presence of dilute sulphuric acid Potassium permanganate oxidizes:
      1. Potassium iodide into iodine.

        2. KMnO4 + H2SO4 + KI → K2SO4 + MnSO4 + H2O + I2

                 

      2. Ferrous salts into ferric salts:

        3. KMnO4+H2SO4+FeSO4→ K2SO4+MnSO4+H2O+ Fe2 (SO4)3

                

      3. Sulphites into sulphates:

        4. KMnO4+H2SO4+Na2SO3→ K2SO4+MnSO4+H2O+ Na2SO4

                

      4. Hydrogen sulphide into sulphur:

        5. KMnO4+H2SO4+H2S→ K2SO4+MnSO4+H2O+S

                

      5. Sulphur dioxide into sulphuric acid :

        6. KMnO4+SO2+H2O→ K2SO4+MnSO4+H2SO4

                

      6. Hydrogen peroxide into water and oxygen:

        7. KMnO4+H2SO4+H2O2→ K2SO4+MnSO4+H2O +O2

                

      7. Nitrites into corresponding nitrates :

        8. KMnO4+H2SO4+NaNO2→ K2SO4+MnSO4+H2O + NaNO3

                

      8. Oxalic acid into carbon dioxide :

        9. KMnO4+H2SO4+H2C2O4→ K2SO4+MnSO4+H2O+CO2

                

    • In alkaline medium:

      Alkaline Potassium permanganate oxidizes Potassium iodide into Potassium iodate.

      KMnO4+KI+H2O→ MnO2+KOH+KIO3

              

    • In neutral medium:

      Neutral Potassium permanganate oxidizes:
      1. Manganous sulphate into manganese dioxide

        KMnO4+MnSO4+H2O→ MnO2+K2SO4+H2SO4

                

      2. Hydrogen sulphide into Sulphur:

        3. KMnO4+H2S → KOH +MnO2+H2O +S
