Reactions of Carbon dioxide:

1. Under high pressure Carbon dioxide gas dissolves in water to form weakly acidic Carbonic acid:

CO2 + H2O → H2CO3

         

Time Taken:00:00

2. Burning Magnesium continues to burn in Carbon dioxide gas to form Magnesium oxide and Carbon:

Mg + CO2 → MgO + C

         

Time Taken:00:00

3. When Carbon dioxide gas is passed into lime water, first it forms milky Calcium carbonate; and when excess carbon dioxide is passed into the milky suspension, it becomes colourless Calcium bicarbonate solution :

CO2 + Ca(OH)2 → CaCO3 + H2O

         

Time Taken:00:00

CO2 + CaCO3 H2O → Ca(HCO3)2

         

Time Taken:00:00

4. When Carbon dioxide gas is passed into Sodium hydroxide solution, first it forms colourless Sodium carbonate; and when excess carbon dioxide is passed into the colourless solution ,it forms a faint white suspension of Sodium bicarbonate:

CO2 + NaOH → Na2CO3 + H2O

         

Time Taken:00:00

CO2 + Na2CO3 H2O → NaHCO3

         

Time Taken:00:00

5.When Carbon dioxide gas is passed over red hot coke(Carbon), Carbon monoxide gas is formed:

CO2 + C → CO

         

Time Taken:00:00

6. At 200oC temperature and 200 atm pressure Carbon dioxide gas reacts with Ammonia gas to form urea (NH2CONH2)and water:

Effect of heat on metallic carbonates:

1. When white Calcium carbonate is heated strongly, it decomposes into white Calcium oxide and colourless Carbon dioxide gas:

Time Taken:00:00

2. When white Magnesium carbonate is heated strongly, it decomposes into white Magnesium oxide and colourless Carbon dioxide gas:

MgCO3 → MgO + CO2

         

Time Taken:00:00

3. When white Zinc carbonate is heated strongly, it decomposes into Zinc oxide which is yellow when hot and white when cold, and colourless Carbon dioxide gas:

Time Taken:00:00

4. When white Lead(II) carbonate is heated strongly, it decomposes into Lead(II) oxide which is orange when hot and yellow when cold, and colourless Carbon dioxide gas:

PbCO3 → PbO + CO2

         

Time Taken:00:00

5. When green Copper(II) carbonate is heated strongly, it decomposes into black Copper(II) oxide, and colourless Carbon dioxide gas:

Time Taken:00:00

6. When Mercury(II) carbonate is heated gently, it decomposes into Mercury metal,colourless Carbon dioxide gas and Oxygen gas:
